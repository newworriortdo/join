Overview

Whales are marine mammals that live in oceans around the world. This site summarizes broad concepts about how whales live, move, and interact with their surroundings. We present neutral, readable explanations without specialized jargon.

Whale Groups & Traits

General Traits

Whales breathe air, produce sounds, and care for their young. Their bodies are streamlined for swimming and vary in shape across different kinds.

Habitats

Whales occupy open waters and coastal areas. Some stay within broad regions, while others follow routes that align with changing conditions.

Behavior

Whales may travel alone or in groups. Common behaviors include surfacing to breathe, changing speed while traveling, and using sound for basic communication.

Oceans & Conditions

Oceans include areas with different depths and temperatures. These conditions shape where whales spend time during various parts of the year and how they move between broad regions.

Conservation Basics

Many efforts focus on maintaining healthy ocean environments. General approaches include reducing disturbances, supporting careful observation, and encouraging responsible boating practices.

Responsible Viewing

When observing whales from shore or a vessel, a respectful distance helps avoid disruption. Calm speeds and predictable movements contribute to safe viewing conditions.

Plain Glossary

Blow

Visible exhalation that appears when a whale surfaces to breathe.

Breaching

A leap or partial rise above the surface, followed by a splash.

Fluke

The tail fins used for propulsion and turns.

Pod

A group of whales that spend time traveling or resting together.
